# Case Study Example — Bahrain IT Crimes Law

> Reference scenario for practicing Law No. 60/2014 (Articles 2–10) case study questions.
> Crimes only — penalties are out of scope per the professor.

## Scenario

Ahmed is a software developer at a tech company that specializes in cloud storage solutions. His company recently landed a major contract to store sensitive customer data for various financial institutions. Ahmed is responsible for maintaining the servers and ensuring data security.

One day, Ahmed receives an email from an unknown source offering him a large sum of money to access customer data stored on the servers. Tempted, Ahmed secretly extracts a subset of encrypted data and provides it to the third party in exchange for payment.

However, he soon realizes that his actions might get discovered. In an attempt to cover his tracks, Ahmed deletes all server logs and alters the timestamps on certain files. Despite this, the company's cybersecurity team notices the irregularities and launches an internal investigation.

During the investigation, the team uncovers the deleted logs and file alterations. The company decides to press charges, and the case is brought before the authorities under Bahrain's IT Crimes Law.

## Question

Based on Bahrain's IT Crimes Law, which specific crimes has Ahmed committed?
